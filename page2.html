Housing & Dormitories

Campus Living

All full-time undergraduate students at Salem College are required to fulfill a four-year residency. Exceptions to this rule are those who live within 30-miles of the campus, or students with dependent children. Those who are exceptions to this rule are able to fill out a residence requirement waiver.

Residence Options

First Year: First years and underclassmen at Salem College primarily stay in the Clewell Hall residence building. This building is built specifically for first-years, though it is possible for underclassmen to reside in any of the other residence buildings.

Upperclassmen: The four other residence buildings include Bahnson House, 1772 Hall, Gramley Hall, and South Hall. These residence buildings house a variety of students of different academic backgrounds.

Amenities

While the amenities for each residence building may vary, there are a few that are consistent throughout them all. These amenities include room furnishings that include a bed, mattress, dresser, chair, closet, and wardrobe. A kitchen with a refrigerator, oven, microwave, and kitchen supplies. Free laundry machines, ice machines, and vending machines, as well as communal lounges and lobbies for guests and events.

Recreational Facilities

Recreation

Salem College has an open-campus layout, connecting with the nearby neighborhood of Old Salem. The campus is abundant with nature and walkable outdoor areas, with plenty of indoor and outdoor activites for students to enjoy.

Indoor Facilities

A few of the indoor facilities offered to Salem College students are the gymnasiums, some for personal use and some located in the athletics center. Some other indoor facilities include the Student Center, which houses both the cafe and the Lucy Rose center. The Student Center offers students a quiet place to study or work on assignments, and on occasion is used to host campus or club events. The Lucy Rose center assists students who plan on pursuing interships or personal career endeavors, offering services like resume workshops or connecting students with potential employers.

Outdoor Facilities

Salem College offers plenty of outdoor seating and event areas. The campus is also closely located to Old Salem, a well-known walkable living museum, and two hiking/biking trails. There are also the outdoor athletic facilities like the soccer, volleyball, basketball, and tennis fields.
